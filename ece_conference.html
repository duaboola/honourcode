About The Conference

Honour Code for Pioneering Educational Training and Translation Services (HC), founded by Al-Shorbaji family is organizing the first-of-its-kind conference on Early Childhood Care Education. The annual conference will be held from 26-28 January 2025 at Golden Tulip Hotel in the Kingdom of Bahrain.
Early childhood education forms the foundation for building strong and educated communities. Therefore, organizing this conference that focuses on the quality of this education is a crucial step. The importance of the firstannual conference on Early Childhood Education, titled “The Impact of Disseminating Quality Education Practices on Implementation in Early Education Institutions” lies in addressing the challenges faced by early education institutions. It emphasises the need to improve education in kindergartens and raise awareness about the importance of early childhood, not only among teachers but also among academics in universities, supervisors, and decision-makers.
Holding an educational conference with the participation of researchers and academics from inside and outside the Kingdom of Bahrain, presenting research papers on real experiences that contribute to raising awareness about early education institutions and specifically supporting the early childhood sector.

Conference Goals

  • Achieve Sustainable Development Goal 4.2 and other related goals concerning early childhood.
  • Exchange knowledge on key enablers, emerging research findings, best practices, and innovations to develop and enhance high-quality early childhood care and education policies and programs.
  • Increase and strengthen regional partnerships and solidarity for the cause of early childhood.
  • Develop teacher training systems in the field of early childhood education.

Conference Themes

  • The role of educational leaders in improving the quality of education: Characteristics of effective leaders in early childhood education and their empowerment tools.
  • Policies for teaching and learning in early childhood education.
  • Enhancing the mental and social health of children in early education institutions.
  • Children's rights and parental satisfaction: Two sides of the same coin reflecting the quality of education in early education institutions.
  • The role of assessment in improving the quality of education: Using assessment results to improve educational practices and make decisions.
  • The role of early education institutions in nurturing gifted children.
  • Equity and fairness in early education: Ensuring all children have equal educational opportunities.
  • Environmental education and sustainable development: Teaching children in early education institutions the values of environmental preservation and sustainable development.
  • Montessori in early education institutions.
  • Quality of early education during crises: How to ensure the continuity of education during crises and disasters.
  • The impact of global changes on early education: The effects of technological and social changes on early education.
  • The role of the local community in supporting early education: Engaging the community in providing a supportive learning environment and organizing awareness programs for families.
  • Supporting children with special needs: Providing specialized educational services for children with special needs and training teachers to work with them in early education institutions.
  • The role of artificial intelligence in personalizing learning experiences for children and developing smart assessment tools.
  • Digital educational games: The impact of educational games on the development of cognitive and social skills in children.
  • Digital safety for children: Protecting children from digital risks and raising awareness among parents and teachers about its importance.
  • Integrating life skills in early education institutions: The increasing role of artificial intelligence represents both a challenge and an opportunity.
Target Audience
  • Educational and teaching leaders
  • Early Childhood Education teachers
  • Directors and supervisors of basic education and early childhood education
  • Researchers and students of early childhood education colleges
  • Parents and those interested in the pre-school education and primary education sector
